Testing Protocol

Six Assays.
Every Single Batch.

Our testing protocol addresses the most consequential failure modes in research compound supply — identity substitution, purity degradation, biological contamination, and trace impurities.

Assay 01

HPLC Purity Analysis

Quantitative purity assessment separating compound from impurities. We report the actual numerical result — not just whether a threshold was met. Minimum acceptance: 99.0%.

Purity Quantification
Assay 02

Mass Spectrometry (MS)

Confirms molecular identity by measuring mass-to-charge ratio of ionised fragments. Purity alone is insufficient without independent identity confirmation.

Identity Confirmation
Assay 03

Bacterial Endotoxin (LAL)

Quantifies endotoxin levels using the LAL assay. Critical for cell-based research where contamination can confound results. EU/mg result published in CoA.

LAL Assay
Assay 04

Residual Solvent Analysis

GC headspace analysis confirms ICH Q3C compliance. Tested for solvents specific to each compound's synthesis pathway.

GC Headspace
Assay 05

Heavy Metal Screening

ICP-MS screens for lead, mercury, arsenic, and cadmium. Quantitative values reported — not pass/fail statements.

ICP-MS
Assay 06

Water Content (Karl Fischer)

Determines water content as a percentage of total mass. Essential for gravimetric measurement accuracy and long-term compound stability.

Karl Fischer Titration
